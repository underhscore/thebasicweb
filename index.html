A Guide To Dashes

What are Dashes

A Dash (or) Dashes is a punctuation mark which has 2 primary ways of using it. The first way of using it to make an interruption in a sentence or express an idea. While the other is used to describe a range of numbers or time. Dashes are often mistaken with hyphens, while (em) dashes are used to seperate ideas/words, hyphens usually join words together.

When we are refering to Dashes, There are two types of them. En Dashes and Em Dashes. (Scroll below for details and examples)

The Two Types Of Dashes

What are en dashes?

En Dashes (–) are used to express a range of time, date or numbers. People often read them as a replacement of the words "to" and "and".

Flip the cards below for an example of how to use En dashes

After

We must study pages 256-273 this Saturday for the test

Before

We must study pages 256 to 273 this Saturday for the test

After

The Roman Empire lasted from 753 BCE-476 CE

Before

The Roman Empire lasted from 753 BCE to 476 CE

Other Uses

En dashes could also represent things without having number values attached to it (as long as they have a realtionship to each other. For example, a vaild use of an en dash without using a number value could be the sentence "The Amsterdam–Berlin train departs at nine o’clock." Or the sentence Clear communication is key to an effective editor–author relationship. . These sentences work because they still make sense with the replaced words ( "to" and "and")

What are em dashes?

Em dashes (—) are interruptions in the middle or end of a sentence. They provide additional information that isn't directly needed to understand a text, they can also be an idea or thought. One key note on the correct usage of Em dashes is that if they're in the middle of sentences you close them off (Closing off —em dashes look like— this.) , while ones at the end of a sentence don't need cut-offs (closing off —em dashes at the end of a sentence looks like this.)

Em dashes can also take the place of parenthesis. Flip the cards below for details on how to use Em dashes.

After

He was wearing a black shirt —not to mention the black jacket— it was truly a change from his normal outfit.

Before

He was wearing a black shirt (not to mention the black jacket) it was truly a change from his normal outfit.

After

There are three cars ready to be deployed — more are on the way as we speak.

Before

There are three cars ready to be deployed, more are on the way as we speak.

Other things to note

Em dashes are are also used to signal a change in thought in sentences. Another example of a em dash could be Faith, hope, and love—these virtues are very important to me.
